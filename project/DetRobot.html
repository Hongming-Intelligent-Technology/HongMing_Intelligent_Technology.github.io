As of the end of 2024, China's railway tunnels have reached 16,798 with a total length of approximately 19,630 km. As the core barrier for structural safety, the quality inspection of tunnel lining concrete is crucial for engineering operation and maintenance. However, traditional inspection methods have long faced three major pain points: first, low efficiency – manual high-altitude handheld radar inspection can only complete 200 meters per day, and setting up inspection frames requires construction interruption; second, data distortion – radar shaking caused by manual operation requires 3-5 repeated inspections to confirm defects; third, high safety risks – fall risks from high-altitude operations and toxic gas threats in tunnels endanger the safety of operators.

To address these challenges, we have developed the "Tiehang Series" adsorption-type concrete defect detection robot over 2 years. Through the deep integration of YOLOv8 deep learning model and adsorption-type robot technology, we realize automated, high-precision, and safe inspection of tunnel lining defects. This article provides a comprehensive analysis from technology selection, system design, engineering implementation to result verification.

I. Algorithm Selection: Why YOLOv8 is the Optimal Solution for Tunnel Defect Detection?

The core requirement of tunnel lining defect detection is the balance between "real-time performance" and "accuracy" – the robot moves at a speed of 0.3 m/s, requiring defect identification and positioning of a single radar image frame within 100 ms, while the recognition accuracy for cavities with diameter ≥5 cm needs to exceed 90%. For this reason, we compared three mainstream detection schemes:

1. Traditional Machine Vision Scheme

It relies on traditional algorithms such as edge detection and threshold segmentation to identify defects through gray value differences in radar images. In actual tests, this scheme has a false detection rate of up to 42% in complex backgrounds such as tunnel water seepage and steel bar shadows, and cannot distinguish similar defects such as "incompacted areas" and "cavities", so it has been quickly eliminated.

2. Faster-RCNN Two-Stage Detection Scheme

Adopting a two-stage process of "region proposal + classification regression", it achieves a mean Average Precision (mAP) of 0.89 on the standard defect dataset, but the inference speed is only 12 fps with a single-frame processing delay of 83 ms. Combined with data transmission time, it cannot meet real-time detection requirements. Moreover, the model parameter quantity reaches 120M, making it difficult to deploy on edge computing devices such as Jetson Xavier NX.

3. YOLO Series Single-Stage Detection Scheme

Adopting an end-to-end regression approach, it converts defect detection into direct prediction of bounding box coordinates and category probabilities. We tested the performance of YOLO versions v5 to v8 in tunnel defect detection scenarios:

YOLO Version mAP@0.5 Inference Speed (fps) Parameter Quantity (M) Small Defect Recognition Rate Edge Deployment Adaptability
v5s 0.88 56 7.5 85% Excellent
v8n 0.90 72 3.2 89% Excellent
v8s 0.92 45 11.2 91% Good

Finally, we selected YOLOv8n as the basic framework and conducted enhanced optimization: its basic version has an mAP of 0.90, an inference speed of 72 fps (single-frame delay of only 14 ms), and a parameter quantity of only 3.2M, with significant lightweight advantages; at the same time, YOLOv8's Anchor-Free design is more suitable for the irregular shapes of tunnel defects. After targeted optimization, the small defect recognition rate can be increased to 92%, perfectly balancing accuracy and speed requirements.

II. Core Technical Solution: Collaborative Design of Hardware and Algorithm

The core innovation of the project lies in the deep collaboration between the "adsorption-type robot hardware platform" and the "YOLOv8 defect detection algorithm", realizing end-to-end optimization from "signal collection" to "defect identification".

1. Detailed Explanation of Adsorption-Type Inspection Robot Hardware System

The robot adopts a "four-rotor auxiliary + negative pressure adsorption" composite structure, divided into two models: "Tiehang-1" (lightweight type, suitable for tunnels with diameter 6-8m) and "Tiehang-2" (heavy-duty type, equipped with multiple sensors, suitable for tunnels with diameter 8-12m). It consists of five core modules:

  • Motion Control Module: Four-drive wheel set + four-rotor propeller, with Mecanum wheels for the front wheel set supporting omnidirectional movement; the rotors provide auxiliary lift, reducing negative pressure adsorption power consumption by 30%. Equipped with high-precision encoders, the walking speed control error is ≤±0.02 m/s.
  • Adsorption Execution Module: Four groups of vacuum suction cups with distributed layout, each with a diameter of 200mm and a single suction force ≥150N; equipped with pressure sensors to monitor adsorption force in real-time, automatically activating backup suction cups when the adsorption force is lower than 80N to prevent falling.
  • Radar Detection Module: 900MHz wireless air-coupled radar with detection depth of 0-1.5m and distance resolution ≤5mm; integrated signal acquisition and processing through timing control chips, with a sampling frequency of 200MHz, generating one radar image frame every 0.5s.
  • Environmental Perception Module: Integrated with lidar (ranging range 0.1-10m), gravity sensor (measurement accuracy ±0.1°), and six-in-one gas sensor (detecting O₂, CO, H₂S, etc.) to perceive tunnel environment and robot posture in real-time.
  • Energy and Communication Module: 50Ah high-capacity lithium battery supporting 4 hours of continuous operation; dual communication link design with 5G transmission rate ≥100Mbps and coverage radius ≥500m, while retaining WIFI backup link to ensure no data loss.

2. YOLOv8-Based Defect Detection Algorithm Optimization

Targeting the particularity of tunnel radar images, we conducted in-depth optimization on YOLOv8n from three aspects: dataset, network structure, and inference deployment, forming a tunnel defect-specific detection model:

YOLOv8 Defect Detection Optimization Flow Chart

(1) Construction and Enhancement of Bimodal Dataset

Traditional single-image datasets cannot cover complex defect types in tunnels. We constructed a "radar image + original signal" bimodal dataset specifically adapted to YOLOv8's adaptive anchor box mechanism:

  • Data Collection: Collected 5km of lining data in Xiaolijian Tunnel (with cavity defects) and Lushan Tunnel (with steel bar abnormalities) of Huhu Railway, obtaining 1,200 radar images (resolution 640×480) and 4,000 original radar signals (each containing 1,024 sampling points).
  • Annotation Specification: Annotated in YOLO format (txt files), defining 4 defect labels: "cavity" (0), "insufficient thickness" (1), "incompaction" (2), "steel bar abnormality" (3). Cross-annotated by 3 senior engineers with annotation accuracy ≥98%.
  • Data Enhancement: Targeting the limitations of YOLOv8's Mosaic enhancement, added a "defect area retention" enhancement strategy, combined with 9 enhancement methods including sliding window cropping (generating 3x samples), mirror flipping, Gaussian noise addition, and brightness adjustment, finally expanding the dataset scale to 15,000 samples.

(2) Lightweight Enhancement of Network Structure

To adapt to edge computing devices, we performed three-step customized optimization on the basis of YOLOv8n, balancing lightweight and detection accuracy:

(3) In-Depth Optimization of Inference Deployment

Targeting YOLOv8's inference characteristics, we adopted a combined optimization of TensorRT + ONNX Runtime to achieve extreme performance on Jetson Xavier NX:

  • Accuracy Optimization: Adopting FP16 half-precision inference, the accuracy loss of YOLOv8n is ≤1.5% (far lower than YOLOv5's 2%), and the inference speed is increased by 2.8 times.
  • Memory Optimization: Utilizing YOLOv8's dynamic shape feature and combining with memory pool reuse technology, reducing model running memory from 0.8GB to 0.4GB.
  • Parallel Processing: Based on YOLOv8's asynchronous inference API, performing radar data collection and model inference in parallel, reducing total processing delay from 150ms to 72ms, far below the real-time requirement of 100ms.

3. Intelligent Monitoring System Software Platform

Developed based on B/S architecture, the software platform deeply integrates YOLOv8 inference engine, supports PC and mobile access, and realizes the full process of "data collection - real-time analysis - visual display - closed-loop management":

  • Real-Time Monitoring Module: Displays robot position (accuracy ±0.5m), posture, adsorption force, radar data and other parameters, embeds YOLOv8 real-time inference window, and supports remote control of robot start/stop, steering, and adjustment of inspection parameters.
  • Defect Analysis Module: YOLOv8 model automatically identifies defect types and positions, calculates defect area, depth and other parameters combined with radar signal characteristics; supports manual review and annotation correction, and generates defect analysis reports with one click.
  • 3D Visualization Module: Constructs 3D model of tunnel lining based on point cloud data, annotates defects identified by YOLOv8 with different colors (red for cavities, blue for insufficient thickness, etc.), supports rotation and scaling viewing, and accurately displays defect pile numbers and ring numbers.
  • Closed-Loop Management Module: Automatically pushes defects identified by YOLOv8 to responsible persons according to severity levels (Grade Ⅰ-Ⅳ), tracks rectification progress, associates re-inspection data, and forms closed-loop records of "inspection - rectification - re-inspection".

III. Engineering Implementation: From Technical Verification to Large-Scale Application

The project has gone through three stages: laboratory testing, field pilot, and large-scale application, overcoming multiple engineering challenges based on the algorithm advantages of YOLOv8, and has now been implemented in multiple major railway projects.

1. Core Technical Challenges and Solutions

Challenge 1: Severe Interference of Radar Signals by Steel Bar Layers

Steel bar meshes in tunnel lining generate strong reflection signals, masking defect features such as cavities and leading to increased model false detection rate.

Solutions: ① Algorithm level: Add a steel bar signal suppression module (based on YOLOv8's custom detection head) in YOLOv8 to separate steel bar and defect signals through Fourier transform; ② Hardware level: Optimize radar operating frequency from 900MHz to 850MHz to reduce steel bar reflection intensity; ③ Data level: Construct a mixed sample library of "steel bars + defects" and conduct special training on YOLOv8's discrimination ability. Finally, reduce the false detection rate caused by steel bar interference from 35% to 6% (better than YOLOv5's 8%).

Challenge 2: Insufficient Stability of Curved Surface Adsorption

Tunnel lining is an arc-shaped curved surface, and traditional flat suction cups are prone to air leakage, resulting in unstable adsorption.

Solutions: ① Adopt flexible suction cup lip design to adapt to curved surfaces with curvature radius ≥3m; ② Develop adaptive pressure adjustment algorithm to automatically adjust the adsorption force distribution of 4 suction cups according to curved surface curvature; ③ Add side guide wheels to make the robot walk close to the wall surface and reduce posture deviation. Tests show that when walking on curved surfaces with curvature radius 3m, the adsorption force fluctuation is ≤±10N.

Challenge 3: Long-Distance Data Transmission Delay

When the tunnel length exceeds 1km, 5G signal attenuation causes data transmission delay, affecting YOLOv8 real-time inference display.

Solutions: ① Deploy wireless relay equipment, setting up 1 repeater every 500m to ensure signal strength ≥-70dBm; ② Adopt data sharding transmission technology, dividing radar images into 256×256 pixel shards and prioritizing transmission of key area data; ③ Local caching mechanism: when transmission is interrupted, YOLOv8 inference results are automatically cached in the robot's local storage and supplemented after recovery. After optimization, the transmission delay is ≤180ms and data integrity reaches 100%.

IV. Future Outlook: Technology Iteration and Application Expansion

Based on the YOLOv8 technical foundation, we have planned three directions of iterative upgrading to further expand product capabilities:

  1. Algorithm Advancement: YOLOv9 + Multi-Source Data Fusion

    Introduce Generative Adversarial Networks (GAN) to generate scarce defect samples to solve the small sample detection problem; based on YOLOv9's higher accuracy characteristics, fuse ground-penetrating radar, laser scanning, and infrared thermal imaging data to build a multimodal detection model, realizing comprehensive detection of "surface defects + internal defects". Plan to complete prototype development by the end of 2025, with defect recognition accuracy increased to over 97%.

  2. Hardware Upgrade: Autonomous Navigation and Endurance Optimization

    Equip SLAM algorithm to realize robot autonomous positioning and path planning without manual remote control; develop wireless charging technology, set up charging stations in tunnels to realize automatic robot charging, extending endurance time to 8 hours. Plan to launch the third-generation product "Tiehang-3" in 2026 with built-in YOLOv8 inference acceleration chip.

  3. Application Expansion: Cross-Scenario Universal Inspection Platform

    Extend YOLOv8-based detection technology from tunnel inspection to bridge, dam, and pipe gallery structural inspection, developing special adaptation modules: bridge inspection module (adding climbing adsorption mechanism), dam inspection module (enhancing waterproof and dustproof capabilities). Plan to build a "Building Structure Intelligent Inspection Cloud Platform" in 2027, integrating YOLO series algorithms to realize unified management and analysis of multi-scenario data.
